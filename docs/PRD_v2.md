## 1. Research Hypothesis
Students understand more, faster, and with greater confidence when an AI explains material in the language they think in, identifies exactly what they are missing, and guides them step by step — rather than simply answering the questions they already know how to ask. Every feature in this document exists to test that hypothesis.

## 2. Executive Summary
Gemma Compass is an AI Knowledge Navigation Platform built for the realities of Nigerian higher education. Its first product, Compass Campus, is a multilingual academic and civic companion for university students. 
Compass Campus takes the material a student already has and turns it into genuine understanding, explained the way a patient senior colleague would explain it in the hostel: in English, in Hausa, or naturally switching between both. It finds gaps in understanding and builds a personal path to close them. **New in v2.0: It now supports full Voice-to-Voice interactions, allowing users to ask questions via audio and receive voice-note responses.**

## 3. The Problem: Understanding Is Rationed
In Nigerian universities, information is not the scarce resource. Understanding is rationed by class size, language, data cost, and social barriers:
1. **One lecturer, five hundred students**: No individual feedback.
2. **Thinking in Hausa, examined in English**: Comprehension happens in one language while assessment happens in another.
3. **The fear of asking**: Formal English acts as a gate; asking basic questions risks public embarrassment.
4. **Data is budgeted in ₦100 units**: Any solution must work offline.

## 4. The Solution: A Patient Senior Colleague, Powered by Gemma 4
- **Reads what you have**: Multimodal understanding extracts concepts from photos/PDFs.
- **Explains like a hostel mate**: Code-switching explanations in English, Hausa, Yoruba, or Igbo.
- **Finds what you're missing**: Adaptive questions reveal misconceptions.
- **Works with zero bars**: Runs locally.
- **[NEW] Audio-to-Audio Interaction**: Bypasses literacy and typing barriers by allowing users to record their questions and listen to the AI's explanation as a voice note.

## 5. Why Gemma 4 Is Core to the Solution
- Multimodal reasoning for real student materials.
- Strong multilingual capability for code-switching.
- Open weights for offline deployment.
- Function calling for structured outputs.

## 6. Target Users
- Primary: University/polytechnic students in Northern Nigeria.
- Secondary: Self-learners, NYSC-bound graduates.

## 7. Compass Campus — MVP Features
1. **Document Intelligence**: Extract concepts from uploads.
2. **Hostel-Style Explanation**: Bilingual text explanations.
3. **Gap Detection**: Adaptive quizzes.
4. **Knowledge Navigation**: Personal roadmap.
5. **Study Planner**: Dynamic revision schedule.
6. **Civic Companion**: Explain regulations/clearance.
7. **[NEW] Voice-to-Voice Chat**: Speak to Gemma in your local language and receive spoken responses.

## 8. Flagship Demo Flow
Photo of a handout page → Gemma 4 extracts concepts → explanation in Hausa (code-switched) → user presses microphone and asks a follow-up question via Voice → Gemma replies with a Voice Note → three adaptive questions → personal learning path. All offline.
